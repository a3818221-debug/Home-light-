Welcome to Rishikesh

The Yoga Capital of the World and Gateway to the Himalayas

Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas on the banks of the sacred Ganges River, Rishikesh is a spiritual haven that attracts seekers from around the world. Known as the "Yoga Capital of the World," this holy city offers a perfect blend of spiritual energy, natural beauty, and adventure.

From ancient temples and ashrams to thrilling white-water rafting and serene meditation spots, Rishikesh provides an unforgettable experience for every traveler. Whether you seek spiritual enlightenment, adventure, or simply a peaceful retreat, Rishikesh welcomes you with open arms.

About Lord Shiv

The Supreme Being and Destroyer of Evil

Lord Shiva, also known as Mahadev (The Supreme God), is one of the principal deities of Hinduism. He is the destroyer of evil and the transformer within the Trimurti, the Hindu trinity that includes Brahma and Vishnu.

In Rishikesh, the presence of Lord Shiva is felt everywhere. The city is considered a gateway to Kedarnath, one of the most important Shiva temples in India. The evening Ganga Aarti at Triveni Ghat is a spiritual spectacle dedicated to Lord Shiva, where devotees offer prayers to the mighty river Ganges, believed to have descended from Shiva's locks.

Many ashrams in Rishikesh hold special pujas and meditation sessions dedicated to Lord Shiva, allowing visitors to connect with the divine energy of this powerful deity.
